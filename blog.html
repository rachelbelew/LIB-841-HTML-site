Reflections from a Library & Information Science Graduate Student

Embracing Lifelong Learning as a Library and Information Professional

By Rachel Belew

In the evolving world of libraries and information science, one truth remains constant: the need for lifelong learning. Whether you're a school librarian, academic researcher, or digital information specialist, staying current is essential—not only to serve patrons effectively but to grow professionally.

What Does Professional Learning Look Like Today?

Professional development in our field has expanded beyond conferences and workshops (though those remain valuable). Some of the most impactful learning opportunities I’ve engaged with include:

  • Participating in virtual communities of practice like AASL or library groups on LinkedIn.
  • Completing micro-credentials in digital literacy, instructional design, or copyright.
  • Exploring emerging technologies from AI in information retrieval to digital preservation platforms.
  • Engaging with peer blogs and podcasts on leadership, DEI, and policy changes.

Why Share the Journey?

One of the goals for this blog is to reflect on that learning journey—not to present myself as an expert, but to foster discussion among fellow learners and professionals.

In upcoming posts, I’ll be sharing:

  • Reviews of professional resources and toolkits
  • Reflections on graduate coursework and field experiences
  • Takeaways from webinars, workshops, and networking

Join the Conversation

I invite you to engage—share your experiences, your favorite resources, or even your frustrations. The library field thrives on collaboration, and together, we can navigate the complexities of information work with confidence and curiosity.
